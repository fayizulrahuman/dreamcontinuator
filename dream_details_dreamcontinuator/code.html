The Library of Unwritten Records

"I was walking through a hall where books were not bound by ink, but by the breath of those who never spoke. I found a volume with my name on it..."

Original Fragment

The pages began to hum as I touched the spine. They weren't made of paper, but of a silken, translucent membrane that pulsed with a faint violet light. As I opened the record, the hall dissolved into a vast, open courtyard where the stars hung low enough to touch.

Every word I had ever intended to say—the apologies left unuttered, the declarations of love swallowed by fear, the quiet observations of morning light—was crystallized here. They floated in the air like glowing embers, swirling into sentences that I didn't recognize but understood perfectly.

A figure appeared at the far end of the courtyard, draped in a cloak of shifting constellations. They held a quill made of frozen lightning. "You are early," they whispered, their voice echoing not in the air, but in the marrow of my bones. "We haven't finished the chapter on your silences yet."

I looked down at the book. The pages were now filling themselves, a frantic scrawling of ink that looked like bird tracks in the snow. It was recording the dream as I lived it, weaving the present into the past until the distinction between what was happening and what had been recorded vanished entirely.
